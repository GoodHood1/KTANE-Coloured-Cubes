On the Subject of Coloured Cubes

Funny flavour text

This module initially contains only a pressable screen, an empty square surface, and three stage-light buttons. Press the screen to start.

Pressing the screen reveals nine colourful cubes, each of varying colours and sizes. Each cube has four values: the red, green, and blue components* of its colour, and its size (RGBS). Sizes** are valued from zero (smallest) to two (largest).

In each stage, select cubes to form sets. A set is a group of three cubes where the cubes' four values are each either all the same or all different. For example, the cubes might all have a red component equal to zero, while their green and blue components, and size, are all different.

Pass three stages to solve the module.

*See APPENDIX-COLOUR for a breakdown of colours into their components.
**Press the screen to view all possible sizes.

Positional Modifications

The cubes do not reveal their true nature; they all modify their displayed properties based on their true positions: rows represent red, green, and blue from top to bottom and the columns are numbered from zero to two, left to right. Each cube has increased by one the value of the colour component corresponding to its row, and increased its size by its column number. All values are kept modulo 3.

For example, a large red cube (values 2002 in RGBS order) with middle-right as its true position, has actual values of 2200.
